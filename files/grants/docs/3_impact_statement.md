## **Impact Statement**

AdaRSS delivers measurable impact by addressing a structural inefficiency in global labor markets: the mismatch between rapidly evolving technology and the long-term durability of workforce skills. As economies digitize and automation accelerates, individuals, institutions, and governments are increasingly unable to distinguish between skills that create sustained economic value and those that quickly lose relevance. This misalignment results in reduced productivity, inefficient education spending, and growing workforce instability, particularly in emerging economies where adaptation capacity is constrained.

By introducing a machine learning–driven framework that classifies skills based on long-term economic stability, AdaRSS enables more efficient allocation of human capital across education, employment, and policy systems. For individuals, this translates into clearer career pathways, reduced time spent on obsolete skill acquisition, and improved long-term employability. For educational institutions and workforce programs, it enables curriculum design aligned with future demand rather than historical trends, improving training effectiveness and graduate outcomes. For employers and governments, it provides a data-driven foundation for workforce planning, talent investment, and economic resilience strategy.

The broader impact extends to global economic efficiency. By reducing skill misallocation and improving alignment between training systems and labor market needs, AdaRSS contributes to higher productivity, lower unemployment friction, and more adaptive economies. This is particularly significant for developing regions, where workforce instability has a direct effect on economic mobility and national competitiveness.

Ultimately, AdaRSS aims to function as foundational infrastructure for workforce intelligence—transforming how societies understand, measure, and plan for skill development. By making skill durability quantifiable and actionable, AdaRSS enables a shift from reactive workforce adaptation to predictive, evidence-based human capital development at scale.
