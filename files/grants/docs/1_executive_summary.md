## **Executive Summary**

AdaRSS is a Career Stability Intelligence Layer designed to solve one of the most critical inefficiencies in the global economy: the misalignment between rapidly evolving technology and the long-term durability of workforce skills.

Across industries, individuals and institutions continue to invest heavily in skills that become obsolete within short cycles, while educational systems and workforce agencies lack reliable mechanisms to distinguish enduring competencies from transient technical trends. This results in systemic workforce instability, inefficient capital allocation in education, and widening skill gaps that disproportionately affect emerging economies.

AdaRSS addresses this by introducing a structured intelligence framework that classifies skills based on long-term economic durability and market evolution patterns. Using machine learning and semantic modeling, the system evaluates skills across three stability classes: foundational competencies that remain durable across technological shifts, emergent skills with accelerating demand, and transient skills tied to short-lived technologies or trends.

Rather than functioning as a standalone application, AdaRSS is designed as an infrastructure layer that integrates into existing educational systems, workforce platforms, government planning tools, and enterprise talent systems. It enables data-driven decision-making for curriculum design, workforce planning, talent investment, and national economic strategy.

The long-term vision is to establish AdaRSS as global workforce intelligence infrastructure—enabling societies to transition from reactive skill adoption to predictive career stability planning. This positions the platform not just as an AI tool, but as a foundational system for economic resilience, labor market efficiency, and future-of-work governance.
