## **Grant Application Abstract (High-Success Format)**

AdaRSS is a workforce intelligence system designed to address a growing structural inefficiency in global labor markets: the inability of individuals, institutions, and governments to reliably distinguish between durable and obsolete skills in a rapidly evolving economy. As technological change accelerates, education systems and workforce programs increasingly produce skill pathways that do not align with long-term market needs, resulting in wasted educational investment, reduced employability, and widening economic inequality—particularly in emerging economies.

AdaRSS introduces a structured skill intelligence framework that classifies competencies based on long-term economic stability and labor market evolution. Using machine learning models and semantic analysis, the system categorizes skills into enduring foundations, emergent necessities, and transient technologies. This classification enables evidence-based decision-making across education planning, workforce development, and policy design.

Beyond classification, AdaRSS provides a personalization and advisory layer that maps individual experience profiles to future skill trajectories, supporting targeted upskilling, career transitions, and workforce mobility. The system is implemented as a scalable infrastructure platform with API-based access, allowing integration into educational institutions, workforce agencies, and government labor systems.

The expected impact of AdaRSS is improved alignment between education and labor market demand, increased efficiency in public and private training investment, and enhanced workforce resilience in the face of technological disruption. By enabling predictive understanding of skill durability, AdaRSS supports more adaptive education systems, more efficient labor markets, and improved economic mobility outcomes.

In the long term, AdaRSS aims to serve as foundational infrastructure for workforce intelligence globally, enabling governments and institutions to shift from reactive workforce planning to predictive, data-driven human capital development strategies.
