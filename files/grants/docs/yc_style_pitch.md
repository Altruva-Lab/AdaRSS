## **YC-Style Pitch**

AdaRSS is building a Career Stability Intelligence Layer that helps individuals, institutions, and governments understand which skills actually lead to long-term, durable careers in a rapidly changing economy.

Today, most workforce systems are reactive. People spend years learning technologies that quickly become obsolete, while schools and training programs struggle to keep pace with industry change. Employers, in turn, lack a reliable way to evaluate whether a skill represents a foundational capability or just a temporary market trend. This creates widespread inefficiency in education spending, hiring, and national workforce planning.

We solve this by structuring skills into a stability framework powered by machine learning. AdaRSS classifies any skill into three categories—enduring, emergent, or transient—based on its long-term economic durability and demand trajectory. On top of this classification layer, we provide a personalization engine that maps an individual’s background to future skill paths and recommends whether they should deepen, pivot, or re-skill.

We have built an early working system that includes a fine-tuned DistilBERT classification model, a semantic similarity engine using sentence transformers, and a full production backend with authentication, API key management, usage tracking, and client dashboards. The platform is already structured as a B2B system with plans, billing logic, and multi-tenant architecture, designed for external integrations via API.

Our wedge is simple: start with developers, analysts, and technical professionals who already make high-frequency learning decisions, and expose AdaRSS as an API that evaluates skill stability and career direction. From there, we expand into educational institutions and workforce agencies that need curriculum and policy-level intelligence.

The long-term opportunity is to become the infrastructure layer for global workforce intelligence—embedding AdaRSS into learning platforms, hiring systems, and government labor strategy tools. As labor markets evolve faster due to AI and automation, every major economy will need a system that predicts skill durability and guides investment in human capital. AdaRSS becomes that system.
