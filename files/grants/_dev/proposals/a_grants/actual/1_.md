# 1. WORLD BANK GRANT PROPOSAL

## Program: Workforce Stability Intelligence System (AdaRSS)

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

AdaRSS is a workforce intelligence system designed to improve labor market efficiency by identifying the stability, emergence, and obsolescence rates of skills across economies.

The platform introduces a **Skill Stability Intelligence Layer** that classifies skills into:

* Enduring Foundations (long-term stable skills)
* Emergent Necessities (rapidly growing skills)
* Transient Skills (declining or volatile skills)

This enables governments and workforce institutions to optimize education investment, reduce skill mismatch, and improve labor market resilience.

This proposal requests support for a **national-scale pilot deployment** in partnership with workforce agencies and training institutions.

---

## 2. DEVELOPMENT PROBLEM

Across developing economies:

* Skills are misaligned with labor demand
* Education systems lag behind labor market evolution
* Youth unemployment is driven by skill irrelevance rather than lack of effort
* Workforce planning is reactive rather than predictive

The economic cost is structural inefficiency in human capital allocation.

---

## 3. PROJECT OBJECTIVE

To improve workforce planning efficiency through AI-driven classification of skill durability and labor market relevance.

---

## 4. INTERVENTION: ADA RSS SYSTEM

AdaRSS provides:

### Core Engine:

* AI-based skill classification (DistilBERT model)
* Stability scoring system for labor skills

### Outputs:

* Skill durability index per occupation
* Workforce trend mapping
* Curriculum alignment recommendations
* Sector-level skill forecasting

---

## 5. IMPLEMENTATION APPROACH

### Phase 1: Data Integration

* Collect regional labor market skill data
* Map job postings + training curricula

### Phase 2: Model Calibration

* Fine-tune classification on regional datasets
* Validate with institutional partners

### Phase 3: Deployment

* Government workforce dashboard
* API integration with training institutions

---

## 6. OUTCOMES (WORLD BANK ALIGNED METRICS)

* Reduction in skill mismatch index
* Improved training-to-employment conversion rate
* Better allocation of public training funds
* Increased labor market adaptability

---

## 7. MONITORING & EVALUATION

* Skill stability accuracy score
* Employment outcome tracking
* Curriculum alignment index
* Workforce mobility rate

---

## 8. SUSTAINABILITY

* SaaS model for governments
* API licensing for institutions
* National workforce intelligence integration

---

## 9. PILOT SCOPE

* 2–5 training institutions
* 50,000+ skill classification samples
