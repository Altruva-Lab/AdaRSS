# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

AdaRSS is a workforce intelligence infrastructure that classifies skills based on long-term economic stability, enabling governments, institutions, and labor agencies to reduce workforce instability and improve long-term employability outcomes.

Across global labor markets, workers frequently invest years into skills that rapidly lose relevance due to technological change. This creates structural inefficiencies in education systems, labor markets, and national workforce planning.

AdaRSS addresses this gap by introducing a **skill stability intelligence layer**, powered by machine learning and workforce analytics, that categorizes skills into:

* Enduring Foundations (long-term transferable skills)
* Emergent Necessities (rising demand skills)
* Transient Skills (short lifecycle skills)

This system enables evidence-based decision-making for workforce development, curriculum design, and national labor policy.

# 2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Modern economies face a structural mismatch between:

* Rapid technological evolution
* Slow educational system adaptation
* Unstable skill acquisition pathways
* Inefficient workforce investment strategies

### Core consequences:

* Rising unemployment among technically trained workers
* Increased retraining costs for governments
* Misalignment between education and labor market demand
* Declining career stability for young professionals

A personal illustration of this instability is reflected in real-world cases where individuals abandon technical careers due to perceived instability in skill relevance over time.
